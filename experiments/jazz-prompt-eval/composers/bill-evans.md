# Bill Evans

## Overview

- Born: August 16, 1929, Plainfield, New Jersey @t[=1929] [^1]
- Died: September 15, 1980, New York City @t[=1980] [^1]
- Primary instrument: piano @t[?]
- Known for impressionistic harmonic approach and lyrical touch @t[?]

## Career

- Classical training at Southeastern Louisiana University @t[1946..1950] [^2]
- Miles Davis Sextet, recorded Kind of Blue @t[1958..1959] [^3]
- First Bill Evans Trio with Scott LaFaro and Paul Motian @t[1959..1961] [^4]
- Scott LaFaro died in car accident, Evans devastated @t[=1961] [^4]
- Continued leading trios until his death @t[1962..1980] [^1]

## Harmonic Innovations

- Voicings in fourths and open intervals @t[?]
- Chord-melody approach with inner voice movement @t[?]
- Influenced by Ravel and Debussy @t[?]
