# ii-V-I Progression

ii-V-I: The most fundamental harmonic progression in jazz — a two-chord setup (ii minor seventh, V dominant seventh) resolving to the I (tonic) chord.

## Structure

- In C major: Dm7 → G7 → Cmaj7 @t[?]
- In C minor: Dm7b5 → G7b9 → Cm @t[?]
- Creates strong sense of harmonic resolution @t[?]

## Usage in Standards

- Appears in virtually every jazz standard @t[?]
- Autumn Leaves contains multiple ii-V-I progressions @t[=1945] [^1]
- All The Things You Are modulates through several ii-V-I cycles @t[=1939] [^2]

## Bebop Treatment

- Bebop musicians added chromatic passing chords @t[~1945] [^3]
- Tritone substitution replaces V7 with bII7 @t[~1945] [^3]
- Charlie Parker and Dizzy Gillespie codified bebop ii-V-I vocabulary @t[1945..1950] [^3]
