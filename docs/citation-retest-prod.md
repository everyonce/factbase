## Analysis

### Net improvement is minimal

The +57 specific citations and +0.6 pp pass rate improvement are almost entirely explained by **new citations added to existing documents** since the baseline run (both runs on 2026-03-14; 41 documents were re-indexed between runs). The scan showed 0 new documents added, but 41 updated — those updates account for the 74 new citations (57 specific + 17 vague).

Accounting for new citations: existing-document specific count is approximately unchanged at ~1,898. The #596 patterns do not appear to have reclassified any previously-vague citations to specific.

### Expected improvements — did they land?

| Pattern | Expected to pass | Observed |
|---------|-----------------|----------|
| `Slack #channel, date` | ✅ | ❌ Not reflected in `citations_specific` |
| Named person + date | ✅ | ❌ Not reflected in `citations_specific` |
| `linkedin.com/in/<slug>` (no https://) | ✅ | ❌ Not reflected in `citations_specific` |
| File paths | ✅ | ❌ Not reflected in `citations_specific` |

### Likely explanation: metric mismatch

The `citations_specific` field returned by `check` appears to count citations containing a full `https://` URL — the same criterion as before #596. The new tier 1 patterns (Slack channel+date, person+date, LinkedIn domain, file paths) may be applied at **question-generation time** (preventing weak-source questions from being created) rather than at **citation-counting time** (incrementing `citations_specific`).

Evidence supporting this:
- **0 new weak-source questions** generated this run (vs. 31 in the baseline run), despite 74 new citations being indexed
- `suppressed_by_prior_answers`: 2,325 (up from 2,255 in baseline, +70) — consistent with slightly more citations being handled before reaching the queue
- Review queue: 5,224 total, 78 unanswered — no unanswered weak-source questions in the queue

### What the 0 new weak-source questions means

Two possible explanations:
1. **#596 is working** — the restored patterns are catching Slack/LinkedIn/person+date citations at tier 1, so no questions are generated for them
2. **Prior-answer suppression** — all vague citations already have answers in the queue from previous runs, so no new questions are generated regardless of tier 1

Given that `suppressed_by_prior_answers` is 2,325 (covering most of the 3,110 vague citations), explanation 2 is the more likely dominant factor. The queue is saturated from prior runs.

---

## Verdict

**The `citations_specific` / `citations_vague` metrics are not the right instrument to measure #596's impact.** These counts reflect raw URL presence, not tier 1 pattern matching. The correct measurement would be: how many weak-source questions are generated on a fresh KB (no prior answers in queue) before vs. after #596.

On this saturated prod KB, the signal is obscured by prior-answer suppression. The 0 new weak-source questions is a positive signal but not conclusive.

### Recommended follow-up

To get a clean measurement of #596's impact:
1. Run `check` on a **fresh KB** (e.g., the Jazz or Volcanoes test KB) that has no prior weak-source answers in the queue
2. Count weak-source questions generated before vs. after #596
3. Alternatively: add a `citations_tier1_pattern_pass` counter to the check output that counts citations matched by non-URL tier 1 patterns separately from URL-matched citations
